WHAT MAKES A COVER "TACTICAL"?

Real talk: a lot of brands slap “tactical” on thin polyester covers with fake MOLLE webbing. That’s not tactical — that’s marketing. A genuine tactical seat cover has three non-negotiable features:

Shop at Bartact →

1. Heavy-duty material. 1000-denier Cordura nylon is the standard. Anything below 600D is just regular fabric with a tough-sounding name. Cordura resists abrasion, tearing, and UV degradation in ways that polyester and neoprene simply can’t match.

2. Functional MOLLE/PALS webbing. The webbing on the seatbacks should follow actual PALS spacing (1-inch webbing with 1.5-inch spacing) so standard MOLLE pouches attach properly. If the webbing is decorative or the spacing is wrong, your pouches won’t lock in.

3. Vehicle-specific engineering. Universal “one-size-fits-most” covers are a waste of money. Real tactical covers are designed for a specific vehicle with proper airbag cutouts, headrest routing, and contour-matched panels. Bartact and Coverking are the only brands that consistently get this right.
