What separates Bartact from every other brand is precision. These aren’t cut from a generic template — they’re engineered for each specific Wrangler generation. The JK covers are different from the JL covers because the seats are different. The JKU rear is different from the JK 2-door rear. Bartact accounts for every variation.

  • JK 2-door (2007-2010): Front bucket + rear bench
  • JKU 4-door (2011-2018): Front bucket + 40/60 split rear
  • JL 2-door (2018+): Front bucket + rear bench
  • JLU 4-door (2018+): Front bucket + 40/60 split rear

JK / JKU (2007-2018)

The JK generation sold millions of units and has the widest aftermarket support. Be careful with the year split: 2007-2010 JK models have slightly different seat mounting than 2011-2018 JKU models. Bartact splits their JK line by year range to ensure fitment.

Budget alternatives: Smittybilt G.E.A.R. ($130-180/row) is the only other brand worth considering for tactical JK covers. Their 600D polyester isn’t Cordura-grade, but the MOLLE webbing is functional and the price is right for a trail beater.

JL / JLU (2018+)

The current-gen Wrangler has redesigned seats with different airbag placement. Do NOT use JK covers on a JL — the airbag tear-away seams won’t align. Browse the complete Bartact JL/JLU collection for gen-specific covers.

The JL also introduced new color options from Bartact, including graphite, ACU camo, and several two-tone combinations. Coverking makes excellent Cordura JL covers too (check Amazon) but without MOLLE.
