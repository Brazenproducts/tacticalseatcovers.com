TACOMA-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

  • Power seat compatibility: 3rd gen Tacomas with the power driver seat need covers designed to accommodate the adjustment mechanisms. Bartact and Coverking handle this; cheaper brands often don’t.
  • TRD Pro headrests: Slightly different shape than standard Tacoma headrests. Verify compatibility before ordering.
  • Access Cab vs Double Cab rear: Completely different rear seat designs. Access Cab has fold-up jump seats; Double Cab has a standard bench. Make sure you’re ordering the right configuration.
  • Heated seats: If equipped, confirm cover compatibility. Thick Cordura can reduce heat transfer slightly.

BUDGET OPTIONS

If Bartact’s price point is too high, Rough Country neoprene ($200-280/row) is a solid non-tactical option that provides water resistance and basic protection. Not Cordura, not MOLLE, but functional for daily drivers that see occasional off-road use.
